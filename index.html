Affective Computing

The more we interact with computers and machines,

the more important it becomes for them to be intuitive and responsive to human emotions and needs. As a bonus, we might come to understand ourselves more in the process.

Connecting Science, Art and Psychology

Affective computing is an interdisciplinary field

incorporating computer science, engineering, psychology, cognitive science, sociology, education, even philosophy and art. We can fully utilize the potential of computing, AI, and human-computer interactions, only if we embrace the complexity of the whole experience of life, not merely computing itself.

Aiding
the Disadvantaged

Affective computing studies and extracts information about the emotional state of a person in certain situations, an important aspect for the interaction between people and the computer. In particular, this study considers people with visual disabilities and their need for personalized systems that prioritize their disability and the degree that affects them.

Advancing
Education
